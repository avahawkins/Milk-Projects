Recipe Two: Marbled Milk Art

Creating milk art is fun way to teach kids about colours, create a beautiful piece of art and use up extra milk! Trying out this milk project will leave you with a colourful piece of paper that can be used to draw on or just hang up on the fridge to admire.

Tip: Don’t let the colours get too mixed up or you’ll end up with a brown mixture!

You Will Need:

  • A Shallow Baking Sheet or Dish
  • 1-2 Cups of Milk
  • Dish Soap
  • Cotton Swab
  • Food Colouring (more colours = more fun!)

Instructions:

  1. Pour milk into your shallow dish
  2. Put a drop of each colour your want to use in different places in the milk
  3. Add a drop or two of dish soap
  4. Dip the cotton swab in a bit of dish soap
  5. Touch the cotton swab to one of the colours
  6. Swirl the cotton swab around to create a marbled or abstract effect
  7. Dip paper into mixture and let it dry completely
